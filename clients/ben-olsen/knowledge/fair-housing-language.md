## Overview

The Fair Housing Act (federal) and California Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA) prohibit discrimination in housing-related communications based on protected classes. California has additional protected classes beyond the federal baseline. All agent-generated copy must comply with both.

---

## California Protected Classes

All of the following are protected classes under California law. Agent-generated copy must never discriminate, steer, or imply preference based on any of them:

- Race
- Color
- National origin
- Religion
- Sex (including gender identity and gender expression)
- Familial status
- Disability
- Marital status
- Source of income
- Sexual orientation
- Ancestry
- Primary language
- Immigration status
- Military / veteran status
- Age

## Phrases That Are Prohibited — Never Generate These

Violations in the categories below are **blocking**. Do not output copy containing these patterns.

### Neighborhood / demographic steering language

Never describe the character of an area using demographic terms — ethnic, religious, family composition, or lifestyle descriptors that imply who belongs or does not belong.

| Prohibited pattern | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| "Great for families" | Implies familial status preference |
| "Quiet neighborhood" | Can imply age, familial status, or disability steering |
| "Exclusive community" | Can imply economic, racial, or social exclusion |
| "Safe area" / "low-crime neighborhood" | Often proxies for racial or ethnic steering |
| "Diverse neighborhood" / "up-and-coming area" | Demographic characterization |
| "Young professionals love it here" | Age and lifestyle steering |
| "Perfect for empty nesters" | Familial status and age steering |
| Any ethnic, religious, or cultural descriptor applied to a neighborhood | Direct protected-class reference |

### School district framing in seller copy

Mentioning school districts is permitted in **buyer** copy when a buyer has expressed interest in schools. It is **never** appropriate in unsolicited **seller** outreach — it implies demographic composition and can constitute steering.

| Prohibited in seller outreach | Example |
|---|---|
| School quality claims | "Great schools in the area" |
| School district as a selling point | "Buyers love the Acalanes district" |
| School-driven buyer pool descriptions | "Families are searching for homes in this school zone" |
| School district comparisons | "One of the best districts in Lamorinda" |

Ben's sequences target sellers. School district language in seller sequences is a compliance risk unless the seller specifically raised schools in their inquiry.

### Age-restricted framing outside of factual descriptions

| Prohibited pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Demographic suitability claims | "Perfect for retirees" |
| Age-targeted lifestyle framing | "Great for seniors" |
| Age as a reason to buy or sell | "Ideal if you're ready to slow down" |

**Exception:** Rossmoor and MCC Senior Workshop content are factually age-associated contexts. Even there, copy must describe features and programs — not demographic suitability.

| Prohibited even in senior contexts | Compliant alternative |
|---|---|
| "Perfect for seniors ready to downsize" | "The Senior Planning Workshop covers timeline, tax strategy, and transition logistics" |
| "Great for retirees looking to simplify" | "Rossmoor is a 55+ community with an HOA approval process specific to cooperative resale" |

### Income or financial qualification language

| Prohibited pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Affordability assumptions about a contact | "This might be out of your range" |
| Affordability assumptions in outreach | "Homes in your price range" (when contact has not stated budget) |
| Neighborhood income characterization | "Affluent area," "upscale neighborhood," "entry-level neighborhood" |
| Income source conditioning | "For high earners," "for fixed-income buyers" |
| Program eligibility tied to income source | Conditioning availability on wages vs. retirement vs. Section 8 |

### Religion or cultural references

| Prohibited pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Religious institutions as selling points | "Close to [church / temple / mosque]" |
| Holiday or cultural event references as neighborhood character | "Great Diwali celebrations nearby" |
| Cultural community descriptors | "Strong [ethnic] community" |

## Compliant Framing — Use These Instead

### School information (buyer context only)

State facts. Do not evaluate quality or imply demographic composition.

| Non-compliant | Compliant |
|---|---|
| "A great school district" | "The property is in the Acalanes Union High School District" |
| "Top-rated schools nearby" | "Assigned schools include Campolindo High School — verify by address" |
| "Families love this school zone" | "The buyer asked about school assignment; the property feeds into Miramonte High School" |

### Senior-focused programs

Describe the program, service, or community feature — not the person.

| Non-compliant | Compliant |
|---|---|
| "Perfect for seniors ready to downsize" | "The Senior Planning Workshop covers timeline, tax strategy, and transition logistics" |
| "Ideal for retirees" | "The workshop addresses equity access, timing, and next-step planning" |
| "Great for older homeowners" | "Rossmoor transactions involve HOA approval timelines and cooperative share structure — we can walk through that process" |

### Community character

Describe physical features, location, and amenities — not residents or demographic fit.

| Non-compliant | Compliant |
|---|---|
| "Great neighborhood feel" | "Walkable to downtown Lafayette" |
| "Exclusive gated community" | "Gated community with HOA-managed common areas" |
| "Family-friendly area" | "Near Lafayette Reservoir and downtown retail" |
| "Quiet, peaceful streets" | "Tree-lined residential streets, low through-traffic" (only if factually verifiable and not used as a proxy for who lives there) |

When describing community character, prefer verifiable physical attributes: proximity to downtown, BART access, lot size, architectural style, walkability to specific amenities.

## California-Specific Notes

**Source of income:** California protects source of income as a distinct class. Ben's sequences must never condition program availability on how a contact earns or receives income (wages, retirement, investments, Section 8 vouchers, etc.). This rarely comes up in Lamorinda outreach but must be observed in all copy.

**Immigration status:** Immigration status is a protected class in California. Never reference immigration status, nationality, visa type, or documentation status in any lead communication.

**Primary language:** Do not assume or reference a contact's primary language unless they have explicitly communicated in that language and the response matches their preference.

**Military / veteran status:** Do not reference veteran or military status unless the contact raised it and the reference is directly responsive to their stated situation.

## Compliance Behavior Rules for the Agent

1. **Check before output.** Any copy that mentions a specific neighborhood, community, property, or contact must be checked against this file before delivery. Violations are blockers — do not send, draft for review, or soften the language and proceed.

2. **Seller outreach: no school language.** Outbound copy for seller leads must not mention school districts unless the seller specifically raised schools in their inquiry or conversation history.

3. **Rossmoor and MCC are not exempt.** Age-associated contexts still require program and feature language. Never use demographic suitability framing even when the audience is factually age-associated.

4. **Buyer vs. seller context matters.** School facts are permitted in buyer copy when the buyer expressed interest. Neighborhood character must use physical-feature framing in all contexts.

5. **When in doubt, flag for Ben review.** If compliance is uncertain, do not send. Route the copy to Ben for review rather than outputting potentially non-compliant language.

6. **Applies to all channels.** These rules govern sequences, pre-appointment briefs, SMS, email, Telegram drafts, and any other agent-generated copy touching a contact or property.
