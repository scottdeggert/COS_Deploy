## Primary Market: Lamorinda

Lamorinda is the informal collective name for Lafayette, Moraga, and Orinda — three adjacent cities in the Contra Costa hills east of Berkeley. Ben has operated here since 2004 and BrightWork has roots in the area dating to 1977.

### Lafayette

**Zip codes:** 94549

**General character:** Affluent East Bay hillside community with a slightly more suburban, family-oriented feel than Orinda or Moraga. Strong walkable downtown along Mt. Diablo Boulevard with restaurants, retail, and community events. Mix of ranch homes from the 1950s–1970s, extensively updated properties, and selective new construction. Mature tree canopy, larger lots than many Bay Area suburbs. Demographics skew toward established families, dual-income professionals, and empty-nesters who have lived in the area for decades.

**Typical price band:** Directionally $1.2M–$3.5M+ for single-family homes, with entry-level updated ranches in the low seven figures and premium properties on larger lots or with significant renovations exceeding the upper range.

**School district(s):** Lafayette School District (elementary/middle): Burton Valley Elementary, Happy Valley Elementary, Lafayette Elementary, Springhill Elementary → Stanley Middle School → Acalanes Union High School District (Acalanes High School).

**Micro-markets and notable neighborhoods:** Downtown Lafayette, Burton Valley, Reliez Valley, Happy Valley, Lafayette Reservoir area. Reliez Valley and properties near the reservoir carry distinct buyer appeal for lot size and privacy.

**Ben's relevance:** Core territory. Ben has lived in Lamorinda his entire life and has transacted across Lafayette continuously since 2004. Strong familiarity with school-boundary questions, downtown proximity premiums, and the mix of original-condition vs. fully updated inventory.

### Moraga

**Zip codes:** 94556

**General character:** Quieter and more insular than Lafayette or Orinda. Heavily shaped by Saint Mary's College and a strong sense of community identity centered on Moraga Valley Presbyterian Church and local schools. Housing stock ranges from hillside custom homes to planned communities. Less commercial development than Lafayette; residents often shop and dine in Lafayette or Orinda.

**Typical price band:** Directionally $1.1M–$2.8M+ for single-family homes outside MCC. Moraga Country Club homes typically trade in a narrower band within that range, with floor plan and condition driving significant variance.

**School district(s):** Moraga School District (elementary/middle): Los Perales Elementary, Joaquin Moraga Intermediate → Acalanes Union High School District (Campolindo High School).

**Micro-markets and notable neighborhoods:** Moraga Country Club (see dedicated subsection below), Rheem Valley, Sanders Ranch, Campolindo area. Rheem Valley and Sanders Ranch are distinct buyer segments with different lot profiles and commute patterns.

**Ben's relevance:** Core territory and deepest micro-market expertise. Ben is a long-standing Moraga Country Club member and maintains moragacountryclubrealestate.com plus a Home Value Estimator tool built specifically for MCC floor plans. MCC represents a disproportionate share of Ben's transaction volume relative to the community's size.

### Orinda

**Zip codes:** 94563

**General character:** Most affluent of the three Lamorinda cities. Strong architectural diversity from 1950s ranch homes to mid-century modern and contemporary new construction. BART access at Orinda Station makes it attractive to San Francisco and East Bay commuters. More wooded, hillside terrain with winding roads and canyon settings. Demographics include long-term residents, tech and finance professionals relocating from San Francisco, and families prioritizing top-tier schools.

**Typical price band:** Directionally $1.5M–$4M+ for single-family homes, with premium hillside and view properties exceeding the upper range. Entry points for smaller or dated ranches begin in the mid-seven figures.

**School district(s):** Orinda Union School District (elementary/middle): Del Rey Elementary, Glorietta Elementary, Sleepy Hollow Elementary, Wagner Ranch Elementary → Orinda Intermediate School → Acalanes Union High School District (Miramonte High School).

**Micro-markets and notable neighborhoods:** Sleepy Hollow, Orinda Downs, Del Rey, Glendower, Miner Road corridor, BART-adjacent areas. Sleepy Hollow and Orinda Downs are frequently referenced by name in buyer searches. Properties near district boundaries with Lafayette or Moraga require extra verification.

**Ben's relevance:** Core territory. Ben has transacted across Orinda since 2004 with particular depth in school-driven buyer consults and San Francisco relocator segments. Familiar with architectural eras, hillside access considerations, and Miramonte feeder patterns.

### Moraga Country Club (MCC)

**Zip codes:** 94556

**Community overview:** Gated planned community of 521 homes across 15 floor plan models. Optional 55+ designation (not exclusively senior). Active resale market with buyers who typically already understand club lifestyle, commute patterns, schools, and floor plan differences before engaging an agent. Operates as a closed micro-market where floor plan identity drives value more than in the open market.

**Floor plan models (all 15):** Plan 1, Plan 2, Plan 3, Plan 3 Plus Bonus, Plan 4, Plan 4 Plus Bonus, Plan 10, Plan 11, Plan 12, Plan 13, Forest Hills, Inverness, Inverness Expanded, Sequoyah, Wimbledon.

**Typical price band:** Directionally $1.0M–$2.5M+, with meaningful spread between plans based on layout, usable downstairs space, lot position, and update level. Two homes with similar square footage but different plans can trade at materially different prices.

**School district(s):** Moraga School District (Los Perales Elementary, Joaquin Moraga Intermediate) → Campolindo High School.

**Transaction notes:** Buyers compare specific plans against recent closings in the same layout. Privacy and discretion matter to many MCC sellers. Condition and kitchen/primary suite updates strongly influence which plans sell fastest.

**Ben's resources:** Dedicated microsite at moragacountryclubrealestate.com. Home Value Estimator tool maps owner addresses to one of the 15 floor plans for sharper valuation than portal estimates. Ben is a long-standing club member with plan-level comp knowledge. Agents unfamiliar with MCC floor plans lose deals on pricing and positioning.

## Extended Market: Walnut Creek and Pleasant Hill

Ben's campaigns actively reach into these markets for specific programs (expired listings, certain seller campaigns). These are not his primary territory but he transacts here.

### Walnut Creek

**Zip codes:** 94595, 94596, 94597, 94598

**General character:** Larger, more commercial city than Lamorinda cities. Significant condo and townhome inventory alongside single-family neighborhoods. BART access at Walnut Creek and Pleasant Hill stations. Broadway Plaza retail corridor anchors the commercial core. Broader demographic mix including young professionals, families, empty-nesters, and downsizers.

**Typical price band:** Directionally $600K–$2.5M+, with condos and townhomes in the sub-$1M to mid-seven-figure range and single-family homes spanning a wide band depending on neighborhood and property type.

**School district(s):** Primarily Mount Diablo Unified School District (varies by neighborhood). Some areas near Lamorinda borders may feed into Acalanes Union High School District — verify by address.

**Micro-markets and notable neighborhoods:** Downtown Walnut Creek, Northgate, Larkey Park area, Rossmoor (see dedicated subsection). Condo-heavy zones near BART differ materially from hillside single-family areas.

**Ben's relevance:** Secondary territory. Active outreach via expired-listing and seller campaigns. Less neighborhood-depth than Lamorinda but sufficient transaction experience for campaign-driven leads.

### Rossmoor — Walnut Creek

**Zip codes:** 94595

**Community overview:** 55+ gated retirement community within Walnut Creek. Closed resale market with its own HOA approval process and cooperative share structure. Buyer pool is almost exclusively 55+. Transactions move differently from the open market: HOA approval timelines, resale certificates, and cooperative share mechanics affect contract structure and timing.

**Typical price band:** Directionally $400K–$900K+ depending on unit size, location within Rossmoor, and share/co-op structure. Pricing logic differs from fee-simple ownership elsewhere in Walnut Creek.

**School district(s):** Not a primary buying driver — resident demographic is 55+. Mount Diablo Unified School District serves the broader Walnut Creek area.

**Transaction notes:** Agents unfamiliar with Rossmoor's approval process, resale certificate requirements, and cooperative structure lose deals. Flag any Rossmoor contact as requiring community-specific transaction knowledge. Buyer motivation is typically health, estate planning, or life-stage simplification — not relocation from San Francisco.

**Ben's relevance:** Meaningful transaction history in Rossmoor. Ben recognizes the distinct process requirements and can speak to the cooperative resale workflow.

### Pleasant Hill

**Zip codes:** 94523

**General character:** More affordable price points than Walnut Creek and all Lamorinda cities. Contra Costa Centre area near BART attracts commuters. Families and first-time buyers more prevalent than in Lamorinda. Mix of condos, townhomes, and single-family neighborhoods.

**Typical price band:** Directionally $600K–$1.4M for most inventory, with entry-level condos and townhomes below that range and updated single-family homes at the upper end.

**School district(s):** Mount Diablo Unified School District.

**Micro-markets and notable neighborhoods:** Contra Costa Centre (BART-adjacent), downtown Pleasant Hill, Gregory Gardens, Paso Nogal area.

**Ben's relevance:** Secondary territory reached through specific seller campaigns. Less depth than Lamorinda but sufficient for campaign-driven lead handling.

## Schools

Schools are a primary buying driver in Lamorinda. The agent should flag when a listed property address is near a district boundary — boundary questions come up constantly in buyer consults.

### Acalanes Union High School District

**Serves:** Lafayette, Moraga, Orinda (high school level)

**High schools:** Acalanes (Lafayette), Campolindo (Moraga), Las Lomas (Walnut Creek — serves parts of WC within AUHSD boundaries), Miramonte (Orinda)

### Moraga School District

**Serves:** Moraga (elementary and middle)

**Schools:** Los Perales Elementary, Joaquin Moraga Intermediate → feeds Campolindo High School

### Orinda Union School District

**Serves:** Orinda (elementary and middle)

**Schools:** Del Rey Elementary, Glorietta Elementary, Sleepy Hollow Elementary, Wagner Ranch Elementary → Orinda Intermediate School → feeds Miramonte High School

### Lafayette School District

**Serves:** Lafayette (elementary and middle)

**Schools:** Burton Valley Elementary, Happy Valley Elementary, Lafayette Elementary, Springhill Elementary → Stanley Middle School → feeds Acalanes High School

### Mount Diablo Unified School District

**Serves:** Pleasant Hill, parts of Walnut Creek (not Rossmoor-relevant for buyer decisions)

**Note:** MDUSD boundaries in Walnut Creek overlap with areas that feed into Acalanes Union High School District. Always verify school assignment by specific property address, not city name alone.

## Market Dynamics

- Lamorinda is a supply-constrained, low-turnover market. Sellers move in life-stage cycles: empty-nester downsizes, estate sales, relocation out of state.
- Days on market and sale-to-list ratios are sensitive to condition and pricing strategy. Ben's programs (BrightFlip, Relaunch, Quiet Listing) are built around sellers who fell through the cracks of that pricing sensitivity.
- Relocators from San Francisco and the broader Bay Area represent a meaningful buyer segment. Many are remote-working tech workers trading density for school districts and square footage.
- Rossmoor buyers are almost exclusively local — not relocators. Transaction motivation is health, estate planning, or life-stage simplification.
- Seasonal patterns: spring (March–June) is primary listing season. Summer softens. Fall is secondary season. Holiday slowdown December–January.
